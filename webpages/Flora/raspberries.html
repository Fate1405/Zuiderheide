Raspberries

Rubus idaeus

Raspberries are a shrub, growing in dense, thorny bushes. They belong to the same family as roses and blackberries. Each stem of these plants can grow incredibly long, reaching up to 2.5 metres in length. Their fruit is edible, producing a sweet flavour. However, it is not recommended that the wild fruit are to be eaten.

Although mainly farmed, these brambles can be found wild in places such as the Zuiderheide.
