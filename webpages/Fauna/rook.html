Rook

Corvus frugilegus

The rook is a bird belonging to the Corvidae family which includes crows and ravens. They are gregarious and common throughout Europe. They nest at the tops of tall trees and rarely migrate.

Similarly to crows and ravens, they possess remarkable intelligence, being able to utilise rudimentary tools to solve problems regarding food. Their voice is usually regarded as a “caw”, not too dissimilar from crows.

They are omnivorous, eating both vegetable and animal matter such as cereals, insects and small mammals. For this reason, throughout history they have been often seen as pests to agriculture and livestock.

These monogamous birds can be seen all around the Zuiderheide but are most easily spotted in places without tree cover.
