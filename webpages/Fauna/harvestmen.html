Harvestmen

Opiliones

Harvestmen, of the order Opiliones, are a type of arachnid closely related to mites and Solifugea. Due to their spindly stature, they are often given the misnomer of Daddy Long Legs, which is often used to classify many long-legged arthropods including crane flies and cellar spiders.

Interestingly, harvestmen are not classified as spiders, but belong to a completely different order. Their mandibles house no venom and are primarily used for chewing. They produce no silk, have only two eyes and, unlike spiders, cannot regenerate limbs after performing autotomy.

They are social creatures and often are seen together in groups. They are harmless to humans and prey on small insects.
