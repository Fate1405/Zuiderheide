Earwigs

Dermaptera

Earwigs (named due to their tendency to crawl into peoples' ears) are small insects of the order Dermaptera (“skin wings” in Greek). The scientific name comes from the shape and appearance of the insect's hind wings, which resemble a human ear. These animals are usually nocturnal and spend daytime resting in small crevices.

Morphologically speaking, these arthropods are small and flat with biting mouthparts, two pairs of concealed forewings and two cerci positioned on the end of the abdomen. These cerci are used primarily as defence weaponry, but earwigs have been observed using them to hold down prey.

An interesting fact about earwigs would be that unlike most arthropods, earwig females care for their young after they hatch even up to their second moulting stage! This behaviour is indicative of the dedication shown by these animals regarding their system of reproduction. After the female dies, the hatchlings will eat her.
